# Hi there, I'm Muktar Ahmed 👋

## About Me
I am a global health researcher, epidemiologist, and data scientist with a strong interdisciplinary background spanning public health, artificial intelligence, health informatics, and biomedical data science. My work focuses on translating complex health data into actionable insights that improve healthcare systems, inform policy, and advance health equity.

I hold a PhD in Genetic Epidemiology from Australia, where my research explored obesity-related and hormone-sensitive cancers using large-scale population and genomic datasets, including the UK Biobank.

Alongside research, I have experience in:
- Clinical and population health analytics
- AI and machine learning for healthcare
- Natural language processing (NLP) for clinical text
- Health informatics and digital health systems
- Epidemiological modelling and biostatistics
- Health services and implementation research

## Featured Projects & Work

### 🔬 NLP for Healthcare
- **AI-Driven Colonoscopy Surveillance System** – NLP pipeline for extracting polyp characteristics, bowel preparation quality, and surveillance recommendations from colonoscopy and pathology reports using spaCy
- **Clinical Report Structuring with Biomedical NLP** – Automated extraction and harmonisation of gastroenterology clinical text using rule-based NLP and machine learning workflows

### 🤖 ML & Predictive Modeling
- **Early-Onset Colorectal Cancer Risk Prediction** – Machine learning models integrating clinical, lifestyle, and population health data for colorectal cancer risk stratification
- **ICU and Surgical Outcomes Modelling** – Predictive modelling and survival analysis for hospital mortality, ICU outcomes, and long-term survival using linked administrative health datasets

### 📊 Health Data Science
- **Geospatial Analysis of Cancer Screening Participation** – Spatial epidemiology and MGWR analysis of bowel cancer screening participation and health inequities across Australian LGAs
- **Health Equity & Population Analytics Frameworks** – Large-scale epidemiological analysis using DHS, UK Biobank, and linked health datasets to investigate chronic disease risk, healthcare disparities, and implementation outcomes
